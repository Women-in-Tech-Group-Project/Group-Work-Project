About:

Ada Lovelace

1815-1852

English Mathamatician and writer.
Published the first algorithm on Charles Babbage's proposed mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.

Edith Clarke

1883-1959

American Electrical Engineer.
She was the first woman to be employed as an electrical engineer in the USA.
Clarke was a pioneer in electricial engineering who used maths to improve our understanding of power transmission.

Grace Hopper

1906-1992

American Computer Scientist and Navy rear admiral.
Hopper led the team that developed the first working code compiler (the A-0 system),
which led to the creation of COBOL—one of the most important programming languages of the 20th century.

Creola Katherine Johnson

1918-2020

American NASA Research Mathamatician.
Johsnson calculated and analyzed the flight paths of many spacecraft during her more than three decades with the U.S. space program.
Her work helped send astronauts to the Moon.

Margaret Hamilton

1936-

American computer scientist, systems engineer and business owner.
Hamilton, a renowned mathematician and computer science pioneer, is credited with having coined the term software engineering
while developing the guidance and navigation system for the Apollo spacecraft.
She was head of the Software Engineering Division of the MIT Instrumentation Laboratory.

Meg Whitman

1956-

American Business Executive,political activist, and philanthropist.
She is the CEO of Quibi, and a board member of Procter & Gamble and Dropbox.

Mitchell Baker

1959-

American CEO of Mozilla Foundation.
Winifred Mitchell Baker is the Executive Chairwoman and CEO of the Mozilla Foundation and of Mozilla Corporation,
a subsidiary of the Mozilla Foundation that coordinates development of the open source Mozilla Internet applications,
including the Mozilla Firefox web browser. Baker orginally trained as a lawyer.

Radia Perlman

1951-

American computer programmer and network engineer.
She is most famous for her invention of the spanning-tree protocol,
which is fundamental to the operation of network bridges, while working for Digital Equipment Corporation.

Kike Oniwinde

1992-

English Business Woman and Athlete.
Kike Oniwinde is a former Great British Javelin Thrower, and the co-founder of BYP Network.
BYP Network is a London based platform that connects over 40,000 young black professionals with each other and corporations.
She was named on the Forbes list of 30 under 30 2019.
